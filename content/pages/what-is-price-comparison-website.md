# What Is a Price Comparison Website? — Consumer FAQ

A price comparison website aggregates product pricing from multiple retailers and displays it in a comparison format, letting you quickly see which retailer has the best price. This FAQ covers how they work, how they make money, and how to use them effectively.

---

## What Does a Price Comparison Website Do?

A price comparison website shows you the same product listed at multiple retailers simultaneously. You search for a product and see:

- **Price** at each retailer
- **Availability** at each retailer
- **Product details** (name, brand, model)
- **Freshness** of the price data

Instead of visiting five retailer websites individually, you see all five options in one view.

---

## How Does a Price Comparison Website Work?

Behind the scenes, price comparison sites operate through:

1. **Data collection**: The site collects pricing data from retailer websites using web scraping, or receives data via retailer APIs and feeds
2. **Product matching**: The site normalises products so the same item from different retailers appears together
3. **Display**: The comparison is displayed with prices, availability, and links to each retailer

When you click a retailer link, the comparison site typically earns a commission (this is how most comparison sites fund themselves).

---

## How Do Price Comparison Websites Make Money?

Most price comparison websites earn revenue through:

### Affiliate Commissions

When you click through to a retailer and make a purchase, the retailer pays the comparison site a commission (typically 1–10% of the sale). This is the primary revenue source for most comparison sites.

### Featured Listings

Retailers can pay to have their products featured more prominently in search results.

### Advertising

Some comparison sites sell advertising to retailers or brands.

The key thing to know: **a price comparison site's ranking is influenced by commission rates, not just price**. The cheapest option might not be shown first if the retailer pays lower commissions.

---

## What Is the Difference Between a Price Comparison Website and a Search Engine?

| | Search Engine | Price Comparison Website |
|--|-------------|------------------------|
| **What it shows** | Web pages | Products with prices |
| **Data source** | Web page index | Aggregated retailer data |
| **Purpose** | Find information | Compare products and prices |
| **Links to** | Any website | Retailer sites (with commission) |

A search engine finds web pages. A price comparison website shows you products with prices from multiple retailers.

---

## How Do I Know If a Price Comparison Site Is Accurate?

Check these signals:

### Price Freshness

Look for a "last updated" timestamp. Prices that haven't been updated in days may be stale — the displayed price might no longer exist.

### Availability Signals

Some comparison sites show prices for products that are out of stock. Look for availability indicators and verify with the retailer before buying.

### Same-Product Matching

Good comparison sites match the same product across retailers. If you're comparing "Apple AirPods Pro" and the results show different products, the comparison is meaningless.

### Coverage

Sites with more retailer coverage give you a better picture of the market. A site that only shows three retailers might miss the best price.

---

## What Are the Limitations of Price Comparison Websites?

### Price May Not Match

The displayed price may differ from what you actually pay due to:
- Stale data (price changed since last update)
- Shipping costs not included in the comparison
- Membership or login requirements for the displayed price

### Commission Bias

Sites that earn commissions may rank higher-paying retailers above cheaper ones. Check all results, not just the top listing.

### Incomplete Coverage

No comparison site tracks every retailer. The best price might be at a retailer not tracked by your comparison site.

### Same-Product Matching Errors

If the site incorrectly matches different products, the comparison is meaningless. Verify that the listed products are actually the same.

---

## How to Use Price Comparison Websites Effectively

### 1. Always Click Through to Verify

The displayed price is a signal, not a guarantee. Always click through to the retailer and verify the price before buying.

### 2. Check All Results

Don't just look at the top result. The best option might be lower down, especially if the site ranks by commission.

### 3. Factor in Total Cost

Add shipping costs to the displayed price before comparing. A site with "free shipping" option might be cheaper overall even if the listed price is higher.

### 4. Check Freshness

Only act on prices with recent timestamps. If a price was updated days ago, verify it before buying.

### 5. Use Multiple Sites

No single comparison site tracks every retailer. Cross-reference with 2–3 comparison sites to ensure you're getting the full picture.
